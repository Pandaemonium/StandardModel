## Core thesis

The manuscript should not claim that we have derived the Standard Model from
octonions. The stronger and more defensible claim is:

```text
We formalize a shared algebraic spine behind several octonionic Standard Model
programs, with explicit conventions and kernel-checked theorem boundaries.
```

The common spine is:

```text
chosen complex structure inside O
  -> complexified octonionic operators and ideals
  -> exceptional Jordan substructures and stabilizer scaffolds
  -> the true Standard Model gauge-group shape S(U(2) x U(3))
```

This paper can tie together three major strands:

1. **Furey**: complex octonions, ladder operators, minimal ideals, charge and
   one-generation arithmetic.
2. **Baez/Dubois-Violette/Todorov**: `h3(O)`, standard `h3(C)` and `h2(O)`
   substructures, complement actions, and the exceptional-Jordan stabilizer
   route to the Standard Model group.
3. **Conventional gauge-group structure**: the finite `Z6` quotient and the
   block group `S(U(2) x U(3))`, which serves as a common endpoint and
   comparison target.

The unifying message is that Lean makes the informal octonionic landscape
auditable: signs, basis choices, parenthesization, quotient kernels, and claim
boundaries are all made explicit.

## Proposed abstract

Many proposed links between octonions and the Standard Model rely on closely
related algebraic structures: complex subalgebras of the octonions, Clifford
algebras generated by octonionic ladder operators, the exceptional Jordan
algebra `h3(O)`, and the compact gauge group
`S(U(2) x U(3)) ~= (SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1)) / Z6`. We report on a Lean 4
formalization of a common algebraic core underlying the programs of Furey,
Baez, Dubois-Violette, and Todorov. The formalization includes an explicit
octonion model with fixed Fano convention, complexified octonion ladder
operators and minimal-left-ideal arithmetic, a coordinate model of `h2(O)`,
a coordinate model of `h3(O)` with Jordan product identities, standard
`h3(C)` and `h2(O)` block scaffolds inside `h3(O)`, and finite `Z6` kernel
packages for the Standard Model gauge group. We also describe reusable formal
transport lemmas for family actions commuting with charge and gauge-table data,
including anomaly-sum invariance under charge-preserving relabeling. We
emphasize precise claim boundaries: the formalization verifies finite algebraic
and coordinate-Jordan statements, but it does not yet prove the full
exceptional-group stabilizer theorem or derive Standard Model dynamics.

### 9. Family-symmetry naturality

Content:

- Introduce the generic theorem.
- Explain why it is motivated by Furey-Hughes triality and nearby `S3`

Informal statement:

```text
If a family action commutes with the charge/gauge operators, then it transports
eigenvectors with the same eigenvalues. Therefore finite anomaly sums computed
from those charges are unchanged under the family action.
```

### 11. Claim boundaries and non-results

This section should be unusually explicit.

Trusted results:

- finite octonion coordinate arithmetic;
- complex-line and complement facts;
- Furey ladder-operator arithmetic;
- minimal-left-ideal finite tables;
- `h2(O)` and `h3(O)` coordinate Jordan identities;
- DVT-style action and complement scaffolds;
- finite `Z6` kernel and block-map scaffolds;
- family-symmetry transport lemmas and anomaly-relabeling infrastructure.

Not yet proved:

- `Aut(h3(O)) ~= F4`;
- `Stab(h2(O)) ~= Spin(9)`;
- the full DVT stabilizer intersection theorem;
- a complete `Spin(9)` or `Spin(10)` centralizer theorem;
- a full Furey electroweak representation theorem;
- a physical three-generation theorem;
- Standard Model dynamics, Lagrangian terms, masses, mixing, or Yukawa
  structure.

The manuscript should make these non-results visible enough that a skeptical
reader trusts the rest of the paper more.
