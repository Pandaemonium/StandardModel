# A friendly primer on Baez's octonionic Standard Model program

Source basis:
- `Sources/Baez_Octonions_Standard_Model_Talk_Notes.md`
- `Sources/Baez_Can_We_Understand_the_Standard_Model_Using_Octonions.txt`
- `Sources/Baez_Standard_Model_Octonions_Lean_Proof_Plan.md`

Status: explanatory primer, not a formal proof and not a claim that the
Standard Model has been derived from octonions.

## 1. The headline

Baez's 2021 talk is not a triumphant "we derived the Standard Model from
octonions" talk. It is more careful and, in a way, more interesting.

The main idea is:

```text
The true Standard Model gauge group S(U(2) x U(3))
appears naturally as a stabilizer intersection inside the symmetry group
of the exceptional Jordan algebra h_3(O).
```

Here:

- `O` means the octonions.
- `h_3(O)` means 3 by 3 Hermitian octonionic matrices.
- `S(U(2) x U(3))` is the actual Standard Model gauge group, not just the
  naive product `U(1) x SU(2) x SU(3)`.
- A stabilizer means "the subgroup of symmetries that preserves some chosen
  structure."

So the slogan is:

```text
The Standard Model gauge group may be the symmetry group left over after
choosing the right extra structures inside an octonionic qutrit.
```

That is the gauge-group story. The harder particle-representation story,
especially the right-handed fermions, is not solved by this route.

## 2. Why octonions enter at all

The octonions are an 8-dimensional number system extending:

```text
R -> C -> H -> O
```

where:

- `R` are the real numbers,
- `C` are the complex numbers,
- `H` are the quaternions,
- `O` are the octonions.

Each step adds more structure and more symmetry, but also loses a familiar
property:

```text
R: ordered, commutative, associative
C: commutative, associative
H: associative, not commutative
O: neither commutative nor associative
```

The octonions are the last normed division algebra. They are strange, but not
arbitrary. They are tied to exceptional symmetry groups such as `G2`, `F4`,
`E6`, `E7`, and `E8`.

For Baez's Standard Model story, two octonionic facts matter most. The first is
that a single imaginary octonion behaves like the usual imaginary number `i`.

A unit imaginary octonion `I` satisfies:

```text
Re(I) = 0
|I| = 1
I^2 = -1.
```

So real linear combinations of `1` and `I` look like ordinary complex numbers:

```text
a + bI.
```

This gives a copy of the complex numbers inside the octonions:

```text
C = span_R {1, I} inside O.
```

This is safe even though octonions are nonassociative. A basic theorem about
octonions says that the subalgebra generated by any one element is associative.
So the 2-dimensional real plane spanned by `1` and `I` really does behave like
`C`.

The second fact is what happens to the rest of the octonions after this complex
line has been chosen. The octonions are 8-dimensional over `R`, while this copy
of `C` is 2-dimensional over `R`. Its orthogonal complement is therefore
6-dimensional:

```text
O = C + C_perp
dim_R(C) = 2
dim_R(C_perp) = 6.
```

The important extra observation is that `C_perp` is not just a 6-dimensional
real vector space. Multiplication by the chosen `I` acts like multiplication by
the complex number `i`. If `x` is in `C_perp`, define:

```text
J(x) = I * x.
```

Then, on this complement, `J` squares to minus the identity:

```text
J(J(x)) = -x.
```

That is exactly what a complex structure is. So the 6 real dimensions of
`C_perp` can be organized as 3 complex dimensions:

```text
O = C + C^3
```

This formula is a friendly shorthand. More precisely, after choosing `I`, the
orthogonal complement of `span_R {1, I}` behaves like a complex 3-dimensional
space:

```text
C_perp ~= C^3.
```

Now look at symmetries. The full automorphism group of the octonions is `G2`.
If we choose `I`, most of that symmetry is broken: only automorphisms that fix
`I` are still allowed. This subgroup is the stabilizer of `I`:

```text
Stab_G2(I) = {g in G2 : g(I) = I}.
```

Any such symmetry preserves the chosen copy of `C`, preserves the complement
`C_perp`, and commutes with the complex structure on `C_perp`. In other words,
it acts complex-linearly on:

```text
C_perp ~= C^3.
```

It also preserves the octonion norm, so it acts by unitary transformations.
That already puts it inside `U(3)`. But octonion multiplication preserves one
more piece of structure on this `C^3`: a complex volume form, or equivalently a
determinant-one condition. Preserving that extra structure cuts `U(3)` down to
`SU(3)`.

The precise mathematical result is:

```text
Stab_G2(I) ~= SU(3).
```

This is why octonions naturally smell like color symmetry: `SU(3)` is the
color gauge group of the strong force. The octonions contain a built-in route
from "choose a complex direction" to "the remaining symmetry is `SU(3)`."

The rough intuition is:

```text
Choosing I breaks octonionic symmetry down to complex-linear structure,
and the remaining symmetry on the 3-dimensional complex complement is SU(3).
```

This does not yet derive quarks or the Standard Model. It is only a symmetry
fact. But it is one of the first bridges from octonions to particle physics,
because the same `SU(3)` that appears here is the group used for color in the
Standard Model.

## 3. Why Jordan algebras enter

Baez does not start with octonions as "numbers particles are made of." He starts
with observables in quantum mechanics.

In ordinary finite-dimensional quantum mechanics, observables are Hermitian
complex matrices. If `A` and `B` are Hermitian matrices, their ordinary product
`AB` is usually not Hermitian, so it is not usually another observable.

But `A^2` is Hermitian. More generally, powers of a single observable make
sense.

This suggests a product on observables:

```text
A o B = (1 / 2) * (AB + BA).
```

This is called the Jordan product. It is:

- commutative,
- generally not associative,
- still good enough to talk about powers of one observable.

The abstract structures with this kind of product are Jordan algebras.

The finite-dimensional "nice" Jordan algebras include Hermitian matrices over:

```text
R, C, H, O
```

with one important restriction: octonionic Hermitian matrices only give Jordan
algebras in sizes up to 3.

The exceptional case is:

```text
h_3(O)
```

the 3 by 3 Hermitian octonionic matrices. This is the exceptional Jordan
algebra, also called the Albert algebra.

## 4. The quantum-information picture: octonionic qubits and qutrits

Baez likes to describe:

```text
h_2(O)
```

as an octonionic qubit, and:

```text
h_3(O)
```

as an octonionic qutrit.

This is analogy, not standard quantum mechanics. But it is useful.

For ordinary complex quantum mechanics:

- `h_2(C)` is the observable algebra of a qubit.
- `h_3(C)` is the observable algebra of a qutrit.

So by replacing `C` with `O`, one gets:

- `h_2(O)`: an octonionic qubit,
- `h_3(O)`: an octonionic qutrit.

The octonionic qubit `h_2(O)` is especially important because it is also a
10-dimensional spin factor. Its determinant behaves like the Minkowski
quadratic form in 10-dimensional spacetime.

So `h_2(O)` carries two kinds of meaning:

```text
h_2(O) = octonionic qubit
       = 10-dimensional spin-factor spacetime-like object.
```

This is one reason the construction feels physically suggestive.

## 5. The exceptional Jordan algebra h_3(O)

An element of `h_3(O)` looks like:

```text
[[alpha, z,     conj(y)],
 [conj(z), beta, x      ],
 [y,       conj(x), gamma]]
```

where:

- `alpha`, `beta`, and `gamma` are real numbers,
- `x`, `y`, and `z` are octonions.

This is a 27-dimensional real vector space:

```text
3 real diagonal entries + 3 octonionic off-diagonal entries
= 3 + 3 * 8
= 27.
```

Its automorphism group is the compact exceptional Lie group `F4`.

That is a huge clue. The Standard Model gauge group is not `F4`, but Baez's
story asks:

```text
Can the Standard Model gauge group be the subgroup of F4 that preserves
some naturally chosen extra structure?
```

The answer reviewed by Baez, following Dubois-Violette, Todorov, Krasnov, and
related work, is: yes, for a specific stabilizer-intersection construction.

## 6. First choice: an octonionic qubit inside the qutrit

Inside `h_3(O)`, choose a copy of `h_2(O)`. Concretely, take the upper-left
2 by 2 block:

```text
[[alpha, z, 0],
 [conj(z), beta, 0],
 [0, 0, 0]]
```

This is an octonionic qubit sitting inside the octonionic qutrit.

The subgroup of `F4 = Aut(h_3(O))` preserving this chosen `h_2(O)` is:

```text
Spin(9).
```

So the first act of symmetry breaking is:

```text
F4
  preserve h_2(O)
Spin(9)
```

This already connects to fermions, because under `Spin(9)` there is a
decomposition:

```text
h_3(O) ~= R + h_2(O) + O^2.
```

The `O^2` piece is the real spinor representation of `Spin(9)`. This is the
space that later becomes relevant to one generation of left-handed fermions.

## 7. Second choice: a preferred complex line inside the octonions

Now choose a unit imaginary octonion `I`. This gives:

```text
C = span_R {1, I} inside O.
```

Once we have this copy of `C`, we can look at the subalgebra:

```text
h_3(C) inside h_3(O).
```

This is the ordinary complex qutrit sitting inside the octonionic qutrit.

The choice of `I` also gives a splitting of the octonionic directions into:

```text
chosen complex line + orthogonal complement.
```

The complement behaves like `C^3`, and left multiplication by `I` gives it a
complex structure.

The subgroup of `F4` preserving the structure induced by this chosen `I` is:

```text
(SU(3) x SU(3)) / Z3.
```

One of these `SU(3)` factors is naturally interpreted as color. The other is
not simply another copy of color. Baez describes it as a larger symmetry whose
subgroup will contain the electroweak `SU(2)` and `U(1)` structure.

## 8. The key intersection

Now preserve both choices:

1. preserve the octonionic qubit `h_2(O)` inside `h_3(O)`;
2. preserve the structure induced by the chosen unit imaginary octonion `I`,
   equivalently the chosen `h_3(C)` and its complex complement structure.

The first condition gives something like:

```text
Spin(9).
```

The second gives:

```text
(SU(3) x SU(3)) / Z3.
```

Their intersection is:

```text
S(U(2) x U(3)).
```

This is the true Standard Model gauge group.

More explicitly, `S(U(2) x U(3))` is the group of block diagonal unitary
5 by 5 matrices:

```text
[[A, 0],
 [0, B]]
```

where:

```text
A in U(2),
B in U(3),
det(A) * det(B) = 1.
```

It is closely related to:

```text
U(1) x SU(2) x SU(3),
```

but it is not quite the same group. The familiar product covers it with a
finite kernel:

```text
(U(1) x SU(2) x SU(3)) / Z6 ~= S(U(2) x U(3)).
```

That `Z6` quotient matters. Baez emphasizes the true group, not merely the
Lie algebra or the naive product.

## 9. The clean algebraic version

The stabilizer story can be summarized using two subalgebras of `h_3(O)`.

Let:

```text
A ~= h_2(O)
B ~= h_3(C)
A cap B ~= h_2(C).
```

Then the key stabilizer claim is:

```text
Stab(A) cap Stab(B) ~= S(U(2) x U(3)).
```

Here all stabilizers are inside:

```text
Aut(h_3(O)) = F4.
```

In the standard block example:

- `A` is the upper-left `h_2(O)` block,
- `B` is the `h_3(C)` subalgebra obtained from a chosen complex line in `O`,
- `A cap B` is the upper-left `h_2(C)` block.

This is the form closest to the Dubois-Violette and Todorov theorem.

Baez and Schwahn later phrase a broader conjecture: perhaps every pair
`(A, B)` satisfying these conditions is equivalent to the standard block
example under the action of `F4`. That would make the construction intrinsic,
not an artifact of a coordinate choice.

## 10. The projective-geometry version

The exceptional Jordan algebra `h_3(O)` also describes the octonionic
projective plane:

```text
OP^2.
```

In this dictionary:

- trace-one projections are points,
- trace-two projections are lines,
- incidence is encoded by the Jordan product.

The subalgebra:

```text
h_2(O)
```

corresponds to an octonionic projective line in `OP^2`.

The subalgebra:

```text
h_3(C)
```

corresponds to a complex projective plane `CP^2` sitting inside `OP^2`.

The intersection:

```text
h_2(C)
```

corresponds geometrically to a complex projective line:

```text
CP^1.
```

So the geometric slogan is:

```text
The Standard Model gauge group is the symmetry group of OP^2 preserving
a CP^2 and an OP^1 whose intersection is a CP^1.
```

That is elegant, but it is also a conjectural/geometric reformulation unless
one has proved the relevant transitivity and stabilizer theorems.

## 11. Where fermions enter, and where the problem remains

Getting the gauge group is not the same as getting the Standard Model.

The Standard Model also needs its fermion representations:

- quark doublets,
- lepton doublets,
- right-handed singlets,
- hypercharge assignments,
- color representations,
- chirality.

Baez discusses work of Krasnov showing that, inside `Spin(9)`, one can
characterize the Standard Model gauge group by its action on:

```text
O^2.
```

Here `O^2` is the spinor piece in:

```text
h_3(O) ~= R + h_2(O) + O^2.
```

Choosing the unit imaginary octonion gives `O^2` a complex structure. The
resulting action looks like the left-handed fermions in one generation.

This is promising.

But Baez is explicit about the hard gap:

```text
The right-handed fermions are not naturally explained by this particular
octonionic-qubit picture.
```

So the situation is:

```text
Gauge group: very beautiful stabilizer story.
Left-handed fermions: promising octonionic spinor story.
Right-handed fermions: still unresolved in this route.
```

That is why our Lean project keeps the right-handed sector as an explicit
claim boundary rather than pretending it has been solved.

## 12. How this differs from Furey's program

Baez's route and Furey's route both use octonions, but they are not the same.

Furey's program is more directly particle-model-like:

```text
complexified octonions
-> ladder operators
-> minimal left ideals
-> number operators
-> electric charges and fermion states.
```

Baez's route is more geometric and group-theoretic:

```text
exceptional Jordan algebra h_3(O)
-> automorphism group F4
-> stabilizers of chosen substructures
-> Standard Model gauge group.
```

Very roughly:

```text
Furey asks: can octonionic ladder operators produce particle quantum numbers?
Baez asks: can octonionic Jordan geometry explain why the gauge group is
S(U(2) x U(3))?
```

Both routes run into the same serious issue: matching the full fermion content,
especially chirality and right-handed fields, without smuggling in the answer.

## 13. What is actually proved, and what is not

Safe mathematical claims from the Baez/Dubois-Violette/Todorov/Krasnov region
include:

```text
h_3(O) is the exceptional Jordan algebra.
Aut(h_3(O)) is F4.
The stabilizer of a standard h_2(O) block is Spin(9).
A chosen unit imaginary octonion gives an h_3(C) subalgebra and extra complex
structure.
In the standard setup, preserving both the h_2(O) block and the h_3(C)-type
structure gives S(U(2) x U(3)).
```

What should not be claimed too quickly:

```text
The Standard Model has been derived from octonions.
The construction explains all fermions.
The right-handed sector is solved.
The three generations are explained.
The dynamics of the Standard Model follows.
Gravity follows.
```

The careful version is:

```text
Octonionic Jordan geometry gives a striking and precise appearance of the
Standard Model gauge group as a stabilizer intersection.
```

That is already a deep target for formalization.

## 14. How to think about the whole picture

Here is the story in one line:

```text
Start with the biggest natural symmetry of an octonionic qutrit, then ask what
symmetry remains after choosing a complex direction and a qubit inside it.
```

More explicitly:

```text
h_3(O)
  has symmetry F4

choose h_2(O) inside h_3(O)
  leaves Spin(9)

choose I in O, hence h_3(C) inside h_3(O)
  leaves (SU(3) x SU(3)) / Z3

preserve both choices
  leaves S(U(2) x U(3))
```

This is the core Baez picture.

The philosophy is not "particles are little octonions." It is closer to:

```text
The exceptional geometry forced by octonions may know the same symmetry
breaking pattern that the Standard Model uses.
```

## 15. How this maps to our Lean project

The formalization should proceed in layers.

Trusted foundational layer:

```text
octonions
complex line inside O from e111
H2O and H3O
Jordan product, trace, trace form
standard subalgebras A = h_2(O), B = h_3(C), A cap B = h_2(C)
```

Near-term stabilizer layer:

```text
define derivations/automorphisms preserving A
define derivations/automorphisms preserving B
prove preserving both preserves A cap B
prove common stabilizers are closed under commutator
```

Medium-term group layer:

```text
formalize S(U(2) x U(3))
formalize its relation to U(1) x SU(2) x SU(3) via Z6
connect standard block stabilizers to this group
```

Long-term frontier:

```text
Aut(h_3(O)) = F4
Stab(h_2(O)) = Spin(9)
Stab(h_3(C)-structure) = (SU(3) x SU(3)) / Z3
Stab(A) cap Stab(B) = S(U(2) x U(3))
left-handed fermion representation on O^2
right-handed-sector resolution or explicit no-go boundary
```

The Lean kernel can verify each formal theorem, but it cannot verify that our
formal theorem is the intended physics. That is why source provenance and
convention notes matter so much here.

## 16. Glossary

`O`
: The octonions, an 8-dimensional nonassociative normed division algebra.

`h_2(O)`
: 2 by 2 Hermitian octonionic matrices. In this story, an octonionic qubit and
also a 10-dimensional spin factor.

`h_3(O)`
: 3 by 3 Hermitian octonionic matrices. The exceptional Jordan algebra, or
Albert algebra. Interpreted informally as an octonionic qutrit.

`F4`
: The automorphism group of `h_3(O)`.

`Spin(9)`
: The stabilizer in `F4` of a chosen `h_2(O)` block.

`S(U(2) x U(3))`
: The true Standard Model gauge group, realized as determinant-one block
unitaries of block sizes 2 and 3.

`Jordan product`
: The symmetrized observable product, usually written `a o b`.

`Stabilizer`
: The subgroup of a symmetry group that preserves a chosen object or structure.

`OP^2`
: The octonionic projective plane, encoded by trace-one projections in
`h_3(O)`.

`CP^2`
: The complex projective plane. In this story it appears as a complex
projective plane sitting inside `OP^2`.

## 17. One-sentence summary

Baez's octonionic Standard Model program says that if we view `h_3(O)` as an
octonionic qutrit, then the true Standard Model gauge group appears as the
symmetry group left after preserving both a chosen complex direction in the
octonions and a chosen octonionic qubit inside the qutrit; the gauge-group
story is beautiful, while the full fermion story remains unfinished.
