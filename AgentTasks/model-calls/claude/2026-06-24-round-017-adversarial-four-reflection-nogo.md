The no-go is essentially vacuous as stated  Your proposed witnesses don't constitute a "diamond" in any sense; they just compare a degenerate loop (`A^4 = I` because `A^2 = I`) to an alternating product (`(BA)^2 = -I`). That gives `2 ≠ -2`, but the same energy is already exhausted by the 2-reflection result: since `trace(R₂R₁)` is a non-constant rational function of the parameters, no length-`n` trace can be constant for `n ≥ 2` (just append `R·R = I` to extend any 2-product to length 4 with the same trace). The "no-go for length 4" is therefore a corollary of what you already have, not a new edge.  A genuine no-go must (a) define "diamond" (closure relation between the four parameters), then (b) show topological invariance fails *despite* closure. Your current witnesses do neither.  ### 2.
