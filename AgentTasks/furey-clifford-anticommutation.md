## Background and mathematical context

The Clifford algebra Cl(6) over ℂ is the central algebraic structure in Furey's
Standard Model construction. It acts on the minimal left ideal J ⊂ ℂ⊗𝕆 and
organises one generation of SM fermions into its irreducible representations.

Cl(6) is generated by 6 elements:
  α₁, α₂, α₃   (ladder operators, already defined and proved nilpotent)
  α₁†, α₂†, α₃† (their adjoints, defined in this task)

subject to the complete set of anticommutation relations:

  Group A — canonical anticommutation (δᵢⱼ relations):
    {αᵢ, αⱼ†} := αᵢαⱼ† + αⱼ†αᵢ = δᵢⱼ · 1   for i,j ∈ {1,2,3}

  Group B — ladder-ladder anticommutation:
    {αᵢ, αⱼ} := αᵢαⱼ + αⱼαᵢ = 0           for all i,j

  Group C — dagger-dagger anticommutation:
    {αᵢ†, αⱼ†} := αᵢ†αⱼ† + αⱼ†αᵢ† = 0      for all i,j

The group B diagonal cases (i = j) follow immediately from the already-proved
nilpotency theorems. The remaining 24 cases are new finite computations.

Together these 27 relations prove that α₁, α₂, α₃, α₁†, α₂†, α₃† satisfy the
defining relations of a Clifford algebra Cl(6), or equivalently, the canonical
anticommutation relations (CAR) for 3 fermionic modes.

**Source**: Furey, "SU(3)_C × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y (×U(1)_X) as a symmetry of
division algebraic ladder operators", EPJC 78 (2018) 375. arXiv:1806.00612,
Section 2, equations after (2.2).

`Scripts/oracle/validate_convention_bridge.py` (extended version). See

## Proof strategy summary

All non-trivial theorems follow the same template:
```lean
ext <;> simp [alpha_i, alpha_j_dag_or_dag] <;> ring
```

- `ext` splits the `ComplexOctonion.ext_iff` goal into 16 real-valued
  component goals (8 for re, 8 for im).
- `simp [...]` unfolds the definitions and applies all `@[simp]` lemmas
  (mul_re, mul_im, add_re, add_im, zero_re, zero_im, one_re, one_im,
  Octonion.mul_c0..c7, Octonion.add_c0..c7, etc.) to reduce each goal to
  a purely arithmetic ℝ statement.
- `ring` closes any remaining polynomial identity over ℝ.

For symmetric cases (e.g. `anticomm_2_1` from `anticomm_1_2`), use
`simp [anticomm_1_2, add_comm]` to avoid duplicate proof work.

If `simp` alone closes a goal (as for `alpha1_nilpotent`), omit `ring`.
If `ring` is unreachable the linter will warn; replace with just `simp`.
